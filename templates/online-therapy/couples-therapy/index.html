Why Online Couples Therapy?

Share at your own pace

Many people have a hard time expressing their feelings aloud. Others need time and space to reflect on emotionally-challenging questions before choosing how to respond. With Talkspace’s Unlimited Texting Therapy™, you and your partner can share your thoughts with your therapist whenever you’re ready.

Skip scheduling headaches

Coordinating a joint appointment for an in-person couples therapy session can be extremely difficult, especially if you have kids. With Talkspace, you’re free to access your therapy sessions 24/7, wherever and whenever you need a place to vent, reflect or respond.

Help that won’t hurt your wallet

Face-to-face couples therapy typically costs $175 per session. Talkspace for Couples offers significant savings—a monthly subscription costs $316, and the quarterly plan is just $852.

How It Works

What do you want to address?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Talkspace?

 

Talkspace is therapy redesigned for how we live today. With Talkspace’s Unlimited Messaging Therapy™, users can access their therapy sessions 24/7, sharing updates and thoughts with their counselors throughout the day in real time. Talkspace offers web access and iPhone and Android apps for reflection on the go. 100% secure and encrypted with the latest in privacy technology, Talkspace users are thrilled about having a safe space to work on themselves without having to travel or spend nearly as much as traditional face-to-face therapy.

Is Online Couples therapy right for you?

 

Many couples find it helpful to proactively enter therapy when they’ve moved beyond the “dating phase” to help set a firm foundation for the future. Every relationship has its own unique dynamic, but for those who are struggling to make things work, common areas of concern addressed in couples therapy include:

Common Areas of Concern for Couples

  • Issues with money
  • Parenting
  • Sex
  • Infidelity
  • In-laws
  • Chronic health issues

Does Couples Therapy Really Work?

 

According to the American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy:

“Research studies repeatedly demonstrate the effectiveness of marriage and family therapy in treating the full range of mental and emotional disorders and health problems.”

Studies have also shown that couples therapy can be more effective than individual therapy for each partner alone. [Source]
